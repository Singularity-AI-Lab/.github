## Building practical, governed AI systems

Singularity AI Lab develops research code and experimental systems for reliable machine learning, governed LLM pipelines, and data-driven energy modeling. Our work connects foundation models with structured constraints, domain knowledge, and measurable evaluation.

<table>
  <tr>
    <td width="33%">
      <h3>Governed generation</h3>
      <p>Role-specialized LLM workflows for producing structured, auditable outputs under domain constraints.</p>
    </td>
    <td width="33%">
      <h3>Energy intelligence</h3>
      <p>Household energy modeling, synthetic consumption profiles, weather-aware scenarios, and downstream analysis.</p>
    </td>
    <td width="33%">
      <h3>Reliable learning</h3>
      <p>Methods for confidence, curvature, annotator noise, and model behavior under uncertain supervision.</p>
    </td>
  </tr>
</table>

## Research direction

We are interested in AI systems that are not only powerful, but also inspectable, reproducible, and useful in real-world scientific and engineering workflows.

- Multi-agent and role-specialized LLM systems
- Synthetic data generation with physical and semantic constraints
- Energy consumption modeling and scenario generation
- Confidence estimation, label noise, and uncertainty-aware training
- Open research tooling that others can reproduce and extend
